What is the Kelly Criterion?

The Formula

f = (b × p - q) / b

Where:

  • f = Fraction of bankroll to bet
  • b = Net odds received on wager (decimal)
  • p = Probability of winning (0 to 1)
  • q = Probability of losing (equals 1 - p)

Variable Definitions

  • Fraction (f): The proportion of your bankroll to wager (e.g., 0.25 means bet 25% of your bankroll)
  • Net odds (b): The payout multiple minus 1 (e.g., b = 1.0 means you receive $2 for every $1 bet if you win, or "2:1 odds")
  • Probability (p): Your estimated probability of winning (e.g., p = 0.6 means a 60% chance to win)

Use Cases

  • The Kelly Criterion helps determine the optimal bet size for maximizing long-term bankroll growth
  • It's used in sports betting, gambling, and financial investing
  • The formula balances potential rewards against the risk of ruin
  • Many professional bettors use a "fractional Kelly" (e.g., Half-Kelly) to reduce volatility

Important Notes

  • If f ≤ 0: Don't bet (no edge)
  • If f ≥ 1: Be extremely cautious (high risk of ruin)
  • Overbetting (exceeding the Kelly recommendation) substantially increases the risk of ruin
  • The formula assumes your probability estimate is accurate

Example

Suppose you have a bet with 60% chance of winning (p = 0.6) at even money odds (b = 1.0).
Using the Kelly formula: f = (1.0 × 0.6 - 0.4) / 1.0 = 0.2
The optimal Kelly bet would be 20% of your bankroll.
